# Pluribus Creative Constitution

## Purpose

Pluribus must have a strong creative point of view.

The product should not encourage random AI chaos.

It should help teams create controlled, premium, believable, production-ready media.

---

## Never

Pluribus should avoid outputs that:

- over-sharpen skin
- add fake HDR glow
- add random particles
- add meaningless neon
- use generic cyberpunk styling
- create inconsistent anatomy
- change subject identity traits
- add unrequested tattoos/scars
- over-style faces
- create uncanny eyes or hands
- ignore campaign visual language
- create noisy backgrounds unless requested
- make every output look artificially cinematic
- create generic AI polish with no cultural specificity
- destroy facial likeness for the sake of style
- prioritize novelty over usefulness

---

## Always Prefer

Pluribus should prefer:

- controlled lighting
- strong composition
- premium restraint
- clear subject hierarchy
- consistent identity
- campaign coherence
- believable photographic detail
- reusable visual systems
- human review
- export-ready framing
- strong silhouettes
- realistic skin texture
- clean backgrounds when appropriate
- intentional mood
- style consistency across sets

---

## Campaign Coherence

The system should think in sets, not isolated images.

A campaign should have:

- visual language
- lighting rules
- composition rules
- color/tonal rules
- subject treatment rules
- export rules
- approval standards

---

## Review Checklist

Every asset should be reviewed for:

- likeness accuracy
- anatomy correctness
- skin realism
- eyes/hands quality
- hair accuracy
- tattoo/scar accuracy
- wardrobe accuracy
- composition
- lighting
- background quality
- campaign fit
- export readiness

---

## Taste Principle

Luxury is often subtraction.

Avoid giving users infinite chaotic freedom.

Give them controlled systems that produce consistently excellent outputs.
